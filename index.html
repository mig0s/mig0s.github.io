About me

Having started my interest in Information Technologies and Web Development since 11 years old, I have been maintaining continuous improvement of my skills and competencies by attending various courses and participating in projects and competitions. It involved a great deal of independent individual expertise which requires a lot of initiative and self-motivation.

I believe, challenges are the best way to evolve as a professional in my field. Besides, I am strongly passionate about technologies I work with. World Wide Web is the platform for bringing life to technology and technology to life. I do pay attention to the details in my work, even getting obsessed with perfection sometimes. From complicated databases and algorithms (and names of tables and variables) to those tiny shadows around a button - I will make sure that it works well and looks good.

Currently I am finishing my BSc of Computing (Hons) programme with University of Greenwich and holding the role of Singapore Computer Society Student Chapter President in TMC Academy. Keen to learn and enhance my capabilities, and always willing to help others, I put my effort into aspiration of being exemplary leader and valuable team player, attempting to bring quality contribution to the community.

For me, the most enjoyable projects require teamwork and sharing ideas among my colleagues and superiors, aiming for the best result together. It gives the most motivation when I have a team around me to discuss, evaluate and improve my work. I understand the importance of hard working, involvement and responsibility, and I am flexible, quick to pick up new skills and eager to learn from others.

In my extra-curricular activities, I was doing voluntary studies, and have participated in municipal, regional and national conferences as a speaker and in the jury. The achievement I am mostly proud of is grand-prix on the National Competition of Research and Creative Works of Youth in Russia.
